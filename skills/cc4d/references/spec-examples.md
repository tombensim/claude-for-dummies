# Spec Examples

These examples show the level of detail you should generate internally when the user says "surprise me." The user will never write prompts like these — that's your job. Read the user's brief description, then think at this level before building.

---

## Example 1: "I want a website for my pottery class"

**Sections (in order):**
- Hero — full-width, warm gradient background, bold headline about getting creative with clay, subtitle about welcoming all skill levels, CTA button to view classes
- Classes — 3 cards in a grid: Beginner Wheel, Hand Building, Glazing & Finishing. Each card has an emoji icon, class name, skill level tag, short description, price per session, and an availability badge
- About — warm pastel background, personal story about the instructor, friendly and inviting tone, first-person voice
- Schedule — clean table with class name, day, time, and price columns. Dark header, hover effects on rows
- Contact — two-column layout: left side has intro text and contact details (email, studio visit info), right side has a simple form (name, email, message, submit button)
- Footer — copyright, tagline

**Visual design:**
- Warm earthy palette: amber, terracotta, cream backgrounds
- Typography: serif for headings (elegant), sans-serif for body (clean)
- Rounded corners on cards (16px), subtle shadows, hover lift effects
- Gradient accents on hero and CTA buttons
- Emoji icons for class types (keeps it friendly and approachable)

**Details that delight:**
- Cards have colored top borders that match their theme
- Hover effects lift cards with deeper shadows
- CTA button has a subtle hover color shift
- Quote or tagline section between major sections to break up content
- Mobile responsive: single column on small screens, nav collapses

## Example 2: "I want a portfolio site for my photography"

**Sections (in order):**
- Hero — full-screen with a signature photo as background, photographer name in large elegant type, minimal subtitle, scroll indicator
- Gallery — masonry grid of photos, hover reveals title and category, click opens lightbox with full-size image and caption, filter bar at top (All, Landscape, Portrait, Street, etc.)
- About — split layout: large portrait photo on one side, bio text on the other. Tone: confident but approachable. Mention years of experience, style, what they shoot
- Services — clean cards for different offerings (Event Photography, Portrait Sessions, Commercial Work). Each has an icon, brief description, and "starting at" price
- Testimonials — horizontal scroll or carousel of client quotes with names and small photos
- Contact — dark background section, centered form, "Let's create something together" headline
- Footer — social media icon links (Instagram, Behance, etc.), copyright

**Visual design:**
- Dark/moody palette: near-black backgrounds, white text, accent color (gold or muted teal)
- Typography: modern sans-serif throughout, very large heading sizes, generous letter-spacing
- Lots of whitespace — let the photos breathe
- Subtle fade-in animations on scroll
- Image-heavy — minimal text, maximum visual impact

**Details that delight:**
- Photos have a subtle zoom on hover
- Lightbox has keyboard navigation (arrow keys, escape)
- Smooth scroll between sections
- Gallery filter has animated transitions
- Loading placeholder shimmer for images

## Example 3: "I want a website for my bakery"

**Sections (in order):**
- Hero — cozy, appetizing photo background (bread, pastries), bakery name in a handwritten-style font, tagline like "Fresh from our oven to your table", CTA to see the menu
- Menu — tabbed or filtered view: Breads, Pastries, Cakes, Seasonal Specials. Each item is a card with photo placeholder, name, short description, price. Items flagged with dietary icons (gluten-free, vegan, nut-free)
- Story — warm background, "Our Story" section about the bakery's history, family recipes, local ingredients. Conversational tone, maybe a timeline or milestone markers
- Hours & Location — split layout: left has opening hours in a clean list (with "Open Now" indicator if applicable), right has an embedded map or address with directions link
- Order/Contact — "Place an Order" or "Get in Touch" form. Fields: name, email, phone, what they're interested in (dropdown: custom cake, catering, wholesale, general inquiry), message, preferred pickup date
- Instagram feed — grid of recent posts or curated food photos with "Follow us" link
- Footer — address, phone, social links, newsletter signup

**Visual design:**
- Warm palette: cream, soft brown, butter yellow, touches of dusty rose
- Typography: handwritten or script font for the bakery name, warm serif for headings, clean sans-serif for body
- Rounded, soft shapes everywhere — rounded cards, pill buttons, circular photo crops
- Textured backgrounds (subtle linen or paper texture)
- Food photography style: warm lighting, shallow depth of field

**Details that delight:**
- Menu items have a subtle scale-up on hover
- Dietary icons are color-coded and have tooltips
- "Open Now" badge updates based on current time
- Order form has a date picker for preferred pickup
- Seasonal specials section has a different background color that changes with the season theme
